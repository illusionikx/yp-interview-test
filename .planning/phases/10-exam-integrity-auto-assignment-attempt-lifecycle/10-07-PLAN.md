<objective>
Ship CLS-07: a lecturer can reset an exam's submissions, behind a warning that states what will be lost.

Purpose: **the warning IS the safety mechanism.** D-2's hard delete is permanent —
`answers.attempt_id` cascades, so graded scores are destroyed with no undo and no audit trail. INT-02
does not forbid destroying a graded result; it forbids destroying one *silently*. The warning is the
entire difference between the two. Treat its copy and its gating as load-bearing, not polish.

INT-03 falls out for free and needs no code: deleting the row releases `attempts.unique(exam_id, user_id)`,
so the student simply starts again through `AttemptController::store()`'s existing `firstOrCreate`.

Output: the reset route, action and Submissions panel — `ResetSubmissionsTest` green.
</objective>

## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| lecturer browser → `DELETE lecturer/exams/{exam}/submissions` | An irreversible, cascading delete of every attempt and every graded score on an exam |
| concurrent lecturer reset ↔ student autosave/finalize | Two writers race for the same attempt rows |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-10-02 | Tampering / Destruction | `ExamController::resetSubmissions()`, the Submissions panel, `reset-submissions` modal | mitigate | Warning-before-act is the mitigation, and it is layered: the stakes appear in the page's summary line **before** the click (with the graded population in red), then in a blocking `<x-confirm-modal>` naming the exact counts, the permanence, and the act in the confirm button itself. Nothing changes until confirmed. The counts come from `AttemptVoider::summarize()` — the single source pinned exactly by `AttemptVoiderTest` — and the toast reports `void()`'s actual return value, so it can never mis-state what happened. Zero-attempt state shows no modal at all (friction proportional to risk). The blocking human-verify checkpoint above covers what PHPUnit cannot: whether a human reads the modal as "permanent". ASVS L1 V11. |
| T-09-01 | Tampering / DoS | `AttemptVoider::void()`'s lock vs `Attempt::lockAndFinalize()` | mitigate | The reset delegates to `AttemptVoider::void()` (plan 04), which takes the same `SELECT ... FOR UPDATE` lock inside one transaction. A racing student autosave/finalize blocks, re-reads, finds its row gone and throws `AttemptVanishedException` (Phase 9, built and tested) — a safe 422/redirect, never a 500. This plan adds no second delete path that could bypass that lock. ASVS L1 V11. |
| T-10-01 | Information Disclosure | — | transfer | Not this plan's surface — closed structurally in plan 06. |
| T-10-SC | Tampering | package installs | mitigate | Not applicable — zero packages installed; zero new Blade components. CLAUDE.md forbids new Composer/npm dependencies and UI-SPEC forbids a third component. |
